# HR Background Check Playbook

## Overview

This playbook guides HR professionals through compliant, thorough, and fair background investigations for employment decisions. Focuses on verification, legal compliance, and ethical considerations.

---

## Legal Framework

### Compliance Requirements

**Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) - US:**
- Written consent required before background check
- Pre-adverse action notice if considering rejection
- Copy of report provided to candidate
- Adverse action notice with rights summary
- Candidate right to dispute inaccuracies

**Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO):**
- Background checks must be job-related
- Consistent application across candidates
- No discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Individualized assessment for criminal records
- Ban-the-box law compliance (varies by jurisdiction)

**State/Local Laws:**
- Credit check restrictions (many states)
- Criminal record usage limitations
- Salary history inquiry bans
- Social media password protection laws
- "Clean slate" record clearing laws

### Prohibited Practices

**Never:**
- Check candidate background without written consent
- Use social media passwords/require account access
- Discriminate based on protected characteristics
- Consider expunged/sealed records
- Check medical/genetic information (GINA)
- Use background check results inconsistently

## Employment Verification

### Standard Verification Checklist

**Previous Employment (Last 7 Years):**

| Information | Source | Verified |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Dates of employment | HR/Payroll | ☐ |
| Job title(s) held | HR/Manager | ☐ |
| Reason for leaving | HR (if available) | ☐ |
| Eligibility for rehire | HR | ☐ |
| Salary (if legal in jurisdiction) | HR/Payroll | ☐ |

**Verification Process:**

1. **Request Documentation**
   - W-2 forms (last 2 years)
   - Pay stubs (recent)
   - Offer letters
   - Separation agreements

2. **Contact Employers**
   - Call company main number (not candidate-provided)
   - Request HR department
   - Verify candidate's claimed employment
   - Document all verification attempts

3. **Handle Discrepancies**
   - Note any differences in dates/titles
   - Give candidate opportunity to explain
   - Document candidate's response
   - Assess materiality of discrepancies

### Gap Analysis

**Employment Gaps to Investigate:**
- Gaps > 3 months in last 5 years
- Frequent job changes (< 1 year tenure)
- Overlapping employment dates
- Unexplained periods

**Acceptable Explanations:**
- Education/training
- Caregiving responsibilities
- Medical issues (don't probe details)
- Freelance/consulting work
- Travel/sabbatical
- Economic/layoff situations

## Credential Checking

### Education Verification

**Verify:**
- [ ] Institution accreditation
- [ ] Degree awarded
- [ ] Major/field of study
- [ ] Graduation date
- [ ] Honors/designations

**Process:**
1. Contact registrar directly (not candidate-provided number)
2. Request official transcript (with candidate consent)
3. Verify accreditation through CHEA database
4. Check for diploma mills

**Red Flags:**
- Institution not accredited
- Degree cannot be verified
- Dates don't match application
- "Life experience" degrees
- Institution has no physical address

### Professional Licenses

**Verification Steps:**
1. Identify required licenses for position
2. Check state licensing board database
3. Verify:
   - License number
   - Issue/expiration dates
   - Status (active/inactive)
   - Disciplinary actions
   - Restrictions/limitations

**Resources:**
- State professional licensing boards
- Nurse Aide Registry (nursing)
- FINRA BrokerCheck (securities)
- State bar associations (attorneys)

### Certification Verification

**Check:**
- Certification body legitimacy
- Candidate's certification status
- Issue/expiration dates
- Continuing education requirements
- Any sanctions or revocations

## Social Media Screening

### Legal & Ethical Framework

**What You CAN Do:**
- Review publicly available information
- Look for job-relevant red flags
- Document specific concerns
- Use consistent screening criteria

**What You CANNOT Do:**
- Require passwords or account access
- Use fake profiles to access private content
- Consider protected characteristics
- Screen inconsistently by candidate
- Use information unrelated to job

### Screening Protocol

**Phase 1: Policy Development**
- Define job-relevant criteria
- Identify red flags for position
- Create documentation template
- Train all screeners

**Phase 2: Consent & Timing**
- Obtain written consent for social media check
- Conduct after initial qualifications review
- Document screening date/time
- Use same process for all finalists

**Phase 3: Platform Review**

| Platform | Job-Relevant Checks |
|----------|---------------------|
| LinkedIn | Professional history, endorsements, recommendations |
| Twitter/X | Public professional conduct, industry engagement |
| Facebook | Public posts (if job-relevant), professional page |
| Instagram | Public content (if creative/brand-related role) |

**Phase 4: Documentation**
- Screenshot relevant findings
- Note date/time of review
- Record specific concerns
- Exclude protected characteristic info

### Red Flag Categories

**Behavioral Red Flags:**
- Violence or threats
- Discriminatory/hateful content
- Illegal drug use (if safety-sensitive)
- Revealing confidential employer info
- Inconsistent with claimed qualifications

**Professional Red Flags:**
- Poor judgment in public posts
- Disparaging previous employers
- Confidentiality breaches
- Unprofessional public behavior

### Documentation Template

```
SOCIAL MEDIA SCREENING LOG

Candidate: [Name]
Position: [Title]
Date: [Date]
Screener: [Name]

PLATFORMS REVIEWED:
☐ LinkedIn ☐ Twitter/X ☐ Facebook ☐ Instagram ☐ Other: _____

FINDINGS:
[Specific, job-relevant observations only]

CONCERNS IDENTIFIED:
[List with screenshots attached]

JOB-RELATED IMPACT:
[Explanation of how finding relates to position]

SCREENER SIGNATURE: _________________ DATE: _______
```

## Reference Verification

### Professional References

**Request:**
- 3-5 professional references
- At least 2 former supervisors
- Colleagues if supervisor unavailable
- No personal/family references

**Verification Questions:**

**Standard Set:**
1. Relationship to candidate and duration?
2. Candidate's role and responsibilities?
3. Strengths and areas for development?
4. Performance in specific situations?
5. Reason for departure (if applicable)?
6. Would you rehire/work with again?
7. Anything else I should know?

**Job-Specific Questions:**
- Leadership capabilities (for management)
- Technical skills (for technical roles)
- Customer interaction (for client-facing)
- Safety record (for safety-sensitive)

### Reference Verification Best Practices

- Call references directly (don't accept written only)
- Verify reference's position at company
- Ask follow-up questions
- Listen for hesitation or coded language
- Document all responses
- Attempt 3x before marking as non-responsive

### Red Flags in References

- Reference can't recall candidate
- Vague or evasive answers
- Hesitation when asked about rehire
- Different story than candidate provided
- Unwilling to provide detailed information
- Discrepancies in dates/titles

## Criminal Background Checks

### FCRA-Compliant Process

**Step 1: Disclosure & Authorization**
- Stand-alone disclosure form
- Written authorization required
- Clear that check will be conducted

**Step 2: Individualized Assessment**

**Consider:**
- Nature and gravity of offense
- Time passed since offense/conclusion
- Nature of job applied for
- Evidence of rehabilitation
- Candidate's explanation

**Step 3: Pre-Adverse Action (if applicable)**
- Copy of background report
- Summary of rights under FCRA
- Reasonable time to respond (5-7 days)

**Step 4: Final Decision**
- Consider candidate's response
- Document business necessity
- Maintain consistency with policy

**Step 5: Adverse Action Notice (if applicable)**
- Final adverse action notice
- Background reporting agency info
- Statement of rights

### Ban-the-Box Compliance

**Check your jurisdiction:**
- When can criminal history be asked?
- Are certain records off-limits?
- What individualized assessment is required?
- Are there certificate of rehabilitation programs?

### Types of Criminal Checks

| Check Type | Scope | Typical Turnaround |
|------------|-------|-------------------|
| County Criminal | Specific counties | 1-3 days |
| State Criminal | Entire state | 1-5 days |
| Federal Criminal | Federal districts | 1-3 days |
| National Database | Multi-jurisdictional | Instant-1 day |
| Sex Offender Registry | National | Instant |

## Credit Checks

### When Permitted

**Generally Allowed For:**
- Positions with financial responsibilities
- Access to sensitive financial data
- Executive/management positions
- Positions with expense accounts

**Restricted/Prohibited In:**
- California (strict limits)
- Colorado
- Connecticut
- Delaware
- Hawaii
- Illinois
- Maryland
- Nevada
- Oregon
- Vermont
- Washington
- Washington DC
- Chicago
- New York City
- Philadelphia

### Credit Check Process

**Requirements:**
1. Job relevance documented
2. FCRA disclosure/authorization
3. Pre-adverse action if negative
4. Individualized assessment
5. Final adverse action if applicable

**Review Factors:**
- Patterns vs. isolated incidents
- Circumstances (medical, divorce, etc.)
- Evidence of rehabilitation
- Time passed
- Job relevance

## Decision Framework

### Evaluation Matrix

| Factor | Weight | Candidate Score | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Employment Verification | 20% | ___/10 | |
| Credential Verification | 20% | ___/10 | |
| Reference Feedback | 20% | ___/10 | |
| Criminal History | 20% | ___/10 | |
| Social Media Review | 10% | ___/10 | |
| Credit History (if checked) | 10% | ___/10 | |
| **TOTAL** | **100%** | **___/10** | |

### Decision Categories

**Green Light (Proceed):**
- All verifications successful
- No disqualifying criminal history
- Positive reference feedback
- No material misrepresentations

**Yellow Light (Caution):**
- Minor discrepancies in employment dates
- Old, minor criminal offense
- Mixed reference feedback
- Requires additional verification

**Red Light (Do Not Proceed):**
- Material misrepresentation
- Disqualifying criminal history
- Negative reference feedback
- Safety concerns

### Documentation Requirements

**Maintain Records For:**
- Disclosure and authorization forms
- Background check reports
- Pre-adverse action notices
- Candidate responses
- Final adverse action notices
- Decision rationale

**Retention Period:**
- 1 year (federal contractors: 2 years)
- Or duration of any related legal action

## International Background Checks

### Challenges

- Varying data privacy laws (GDPR, etc.)
- Different record-keeping systems
- Language barriers
- Longer turnaround times
- Limited criminal record access

### Process

1. **Identify Countries**
   - Candidate's countries of residence
   - Employment history locations
   - Education institutions

2. **Legal Review**
   - Check local data protection laws
   - Determine what checks are permissible
   - Identify required candidate consents

3. **Engage Local Providers**
   - Use FCRA-compliant international services
   - Verify provider credentials
   - Understand limitations

4. **Additional Verification**
   - International education verification
   - Cross-border employment checks
   - Global sanctions/watch lists

## Ongoing Monitoring (Post-Hire)

### Continuous Background Checks

**Common Practice For:**
- Safety-sensitive positions
- Financial services
- Healthcare
- Transportation
- Education

**Process:**
- Annual re-check authorization
- Continuous criminal monitoring
- License/certification expiration tracking
- Driving record monitoring (if applicable)

**Legal Considerations:**
- Ongoing consent requirements
- State law variations
- Disclosure obligations
- Adverse action procedures still apply

---

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Consistency** - Apply same standards to all candidates
2. **Job-Relatedness** - Only check job-relevant factors
3. **Documentation** - Record every step and decision
4. **Compliance** - Stay current with federal/state/local laws
5. **Individualization** - Assess each candidate holistically
6. **Transparency** - Clear communication with candidates
7. **Security** - Protect candidate data
8. **Training** - Ensure all staff understand procedures
