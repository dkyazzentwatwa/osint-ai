# Private Investigator Playbook

## Overview

This playbook provides licensed private investigators with systematic procedures for locating subjects, discovering assets, mapping relationships, and maintaining legal compliance. Emphasizes ethical boundaries and legal requirements.

---

## Legal Framework & Licensing

### Licensing Requirements (US)

**State Variations:**
- All states except Idaho, Mississippi, South Dakota, Wyoming require licensing
- Requirements vary by state (experience, education, examination)
- Some states require surety bonds
- Continuing education often required

**General Requirements:**
- Minimum age (usually 18-25)
- Clean criminal record
- Relevant experience or education
- Passing licensing examination
- Background investigation
- Business license (if applicable)

### Legal Boundaries

**Permissible Activities:**
- ✅ Public records research
- ✅ Surveillance from public spaces
- ✅ Skip tracing (locating individuals)
- ✅ Asset searches via public records
- ✅ Witness interviews
- ✅ Background investigations
- ✅ Process serving

**Prohibited Activities:**
- ❌ Trespassing on private property
- ❌ Wiretapping or electronic eavesdropping
- ❌ Hacking or unauthorized computer access
- ❌ Obtaining phone/credit records without consent
- ❌ Impersonating law enforcement
- ❌ Mail theft or tampering
- ❌ GPS tracking without consent (varies by state)
- ❌ Pre-texting (obtaining info under false pretenses - illegal federally)

**Gray Areas (Consult Attorney):**
- Social media monitoring
- Trash pulls (varies by jurisdiction)
- Recording conversations (one-party vs. two-party consent states)
- Database access restrictions
- GPS tracking (consent requirements vary)

## Subject Locating (Skip Tracing)

### Information Gathering

**Basic Identifiers:**
- Full legal name (including middle, suffixes)
- Date of birth
- Social Security Number (if legally obtained)
- Last known address
- Physical description
- Photograph

**Associates Network:**
- Family members
- Spouse/ex-spouse
- Children
- Close friends
- Employers
- Neighbors
- Professional contacts

**Digital Footprint:**
- Email addresses
- Social media accounts
- Online usernames/handles
- Dating profiles
- Gaming accounts
- Forum participation

### Locating Techniques

**Phase 1: Database Searches**
```
PUBLIC RECORDS:
- Property records (county assessor)
- Voter registration
- Professional licenses
- Court records (civil, criminal, family)
- Marriage/divorce records
- Business registrations
- UCC filings
- Bankruptcy records

COMMERCIAL DATABASES (Licensed Access):
- Credit headers (limited access)
- Utility connections
- Phone directories
- Motor vehicle records (state restrictions)
- Professional databases
```

**Phase 2: Digital Investigation**
```
SOCIAL MEDIA ANALYSIS:
- Profile location fields
- Photo geolocation
- Check-ins and tags
- Friend/follower networks
- Recent activity patterns
- Employment mentions

ONLINE PRESENCE:
- Username searches across platforms
- Reverse image search
- Domain registrations
- Public records aggregation sites
- People search engines
```

**Phase 3: Physical Investigation**
```
ADDRESS VERIFICATION:
- Drive-by of last known address
- Neighbor interviews
- Mail check (if legal)
- Landlord/property manager contact

ASSOCIATE CONTACT:
- Family interviews
- Employer contact (careful with employment law)
- Friend networks
- Professional contacts
```

### Skip Tracing Checklist

- [ ] Verify full legal name and aliases
- [ ] Check last known address (property records)
- [ ] Search court records for recent activity
- [ ] Review social media for location clues
- [ ] Check professional licensing boards
- [ ] Search voter registration databases
- [ ] Review UCC filings for business connections
- [ ] Check marriage/divorce records
- [ ] Monitor social media for activity
- [ ] Interview family members (if appropriate)
- [ ] Verify employment (if relevant to case)
- [ ] Check incarceration records
- [ ] Review death records (Social Security Death Index)
- [ ] Search unclaimed property databases
- [ ] Check professional association directories

## Asset Discovery

### Asset Categories

**Real Property:**
- Residential real estate
- Commercial properties
- Vacant land
- Out-of-state properties
- Properties in LLC/corporate names
- Recently sold properties

**Personal Property:**
- Vehicles (registered, titled)
- Watercraft
- Aircraft
- Luxury items (art, jewelry, collectibles)
- Business equipment

**Financial Assets:**
- Bank accounts (difficult to locate legally)
- Investment accounts
- Retirement accounts
- Trust interests
- Insurance policies (cash value)
- Cryptocurrency holdings

**Business Interests:**
- Ownership in corporations/LLCs
- Partnership interests
- Sole proprietorships
- Professional practices
- Franchise interests

**Employment/Benefits:**
- Current employment
- Salary/wages
- Commission structures
- Bonus arrangements
- Stock options
- Pension plans

### Asset Search Process

**Real Property Search:**
```
1. County Assessor Records
   - Search grantor/grantee indexes
   - Check property tax records
   - Review deed transfers
   - Identify properties in trust/LLC names

2. Statewide Searches (if available)
   - Some states have centralized databases
   - Multi-county search services

3. Pattern Analysis
   - Look for properties in family member names
   - Recent transfers (fraudulent conveyance?)
   - Out-of-state property ownership
```

**Vehicle/Asset Search:**
```
1. DMV Records (State Restrictions Apply)
   - Registered vehicles
   - Title transfers
   - Lienholder information

2. UCC Filings
   - Secured loans (indicates assets)
   - Equipment financing
   - Inventory liens

3. Marine/Aviation Records
   - Coast Guard vessel documentation
   - FAA aircraft registry
```

**Business Interest Search:**
```
1. Secretary of State Filings
   - Corporation registrations
   - LLC formations
   - Partnership filings
   - Registered agent information

2. Assumed Name/DBA Records
   - County clerk filings
   - Trade name registrations

3. Professional Licensing Boards
   - Professional practice ownership
   - License status
   - Disciplinary records
```

### Asset Search Limitations

**Difficult to Locate Legally:**
- Bank account information (requires legal process)
- Investment accounts (limited public data)
- Offshore assets (jurisdictional challenges)
- Cryptocurrency (pseudo-anonymous)
- Assets in other people's names

**Warning Signs:**
- Recent property transfers to family
- Business dissolution or formation
- Name changes
- Foreign entity formations
- Complex trust structures

## Relationship Mapping

### Network Analysis

**Relationship Types:**
```
FAMILY:
- Spouse/current partner
- Ex-spouses
- Children
- Parents
- Siblings
- Extended family

PROFESSIONAL:
- Employers/businesses
- Business partners
- Clients/customers
- Professional associates
- Competitors

SOCIAL:
- Close friends
- Social club memberships
- Religious affiliations
- Hobby/interest groups
- Online communities

FINANCIAL:
- Co-signers on loans
- Business partners
- Joint account holders
- Creditors
- Debtors
```

### Mapping Process

**Step 1: Identify Key Relationships**
```
SOURCES:
- Social media friend lists
- Tag analysis in photos
- Co-occurrence in public records
- Business partnership filings
- Property co-ownership
- Court case co-parties
- Professional associations
```

**Step 2: Analyze Relationship Strength**
```
INDICATORS:
- Frequency of interaction (social media)
- Reciprocity of connection
- Shared addresses/properties
- Joint business ventures
- Co-signatures on documents
- Time known (if discoverable)
- Communication patterns
```

**Step 3: Identify Information Sources**
```
Each relationship is a potential:
- Location lead (where subject might be)
- Asset lead (what subject might own)
- Activity lead (what subject is doing)
- Background information source
- Potential witness/interview
```

## Surveillance Preparation

### Pre-Surveillance Checklist

**Legal Requirements:**
- [ ] Verify surveillance is legal in jurisdiction
- [ ] Confirm no expectation of privacy violation
- [ ] Check for restraining orders
- [ ] Review client authorization scope
- [ ] Confirm no conflict of interest

**Subject Intelligence:**
- [ ] Current photograph
- [ ] Physical description (height, weight, hair, eyes, distinguishing marks)
- [ ] Vehicle description (make, model, color, plate)
- [ ] Known associates who might be present
- [ ] Work schedule (if known)
- [ ] Frequent locations
- [ ] Habits and routines

**Site Preparation:**
- [ ] Pre-surveillance of location
- [ ] Identify observation positions
- [ ] Note lighting conditions by time of day
- [ ] Identify parking for surveillance vehicle
- [ ] Check for security cameras
- [ ] Note entry/exit points
- [ ] Identify alternative positions
- [ ] Check cell phone coverage

### Equipment Check

**Essential Equipment:**
- [ ] Video camera (preferably 4K)
- [ ] DSLR camera with zoom lens
- [ ] Binoculars
- [ ] GPS unit
- [ ] Audio recorder (for notes, not subject recording)
- [ ] Two-way radio (if team operation)
- [ ] Laptop/tablet for database access
- [ ] Binoculars
- [ ] Sunglasses/hat for discretion
- [ ] Change of clothes (if needed)

**Vehicle Preparation:**
- [ ] Full fuel tank
- [ ] Discreet appearance
- [ ] Tinted windows (legal limit)
- [ ] Phone charger
- [ ] Cooler with drinks/snacks
- [ ] Emergency supplies
- [ ] Maps/GPS of area

### Documentation Standards

**Surveillance Log Requirements:**
```
DATE: [Date of surveillance]
INVESTIGATOR: [Name, License #]
CLIENT: [Case reference]
SUBJECT: [Name, Description]

TIME | ACTIVITY | LOCATION | WITNESSES | EVIDENCE
-----|----------|----------|-----------|----------
08:00| [Activity]|[Address] | [Who saw] | [Photo/video ref]
```

**Evidence Standards:**
- Timestamp all video/photos
- Maintain chain of custody
- Use contemporaneous notes
- Include environmental context
- Document weather/lighting
- Note any interactions with subject

## Legal Boundaries

### Privacy Expectations

**No Expectation of Privacy (Generally):**
- Activities in public spaces
- Visible through windows from public areas
- Posted on public social media
- Public records
- License plates in public
- Conversations in public (loud enough to overhear)

**Reasonable Expectation of Privacy (Generally Protected):**
- Private residences (interior)
- Private conversations
- Phone calls
- Mail
- Medical records
- Financial records
- Location inside private property

### State-by-State Variations

**GPS Tracking Laws:**
- Some states require consent of vehicle owner
- Some prohibit without warrant
- Federal law: generally requires consent

**Recording Conversations:**
- One-party consent states: 38 states + DC
- Two-party (all-party) consent: 12 states
- Know your state's law

**Database Access:**
- DMV records vary by state
- Some records restricted to law enforcement
- GLBA, DPPA restrictions apply

### Ethics Guidelines

**PI Code of Ethics (General):**
1. Conduct business with integrity
2. Obey all laws and regulations
3. Maintain client confidentiality
4. Avoid conflicts of interest
5. Represent qualifications honestly
6. Treat subjects with dignity
7. Report findings accurately
8. Preserve evidence properly
9. Continue professional education
10. Uphold profession's reputation

## Report Requirements

### Standard Report Sections

**1. Case Summary:**
- Client identification
- Investigation objectives
- Date range of investigation
- Summary of findings

**2. Methodology:**
- Investigative techniques used
- Databases searched
- Interviews conducted
- Surveillance conducted
- Legal compliance statement

**3. Findings:**
- Subject location (if found)
- Asset identification
- Relationship mapping
- Activity documentation
- Timeline of events

**4. Evidence:**
- Photographs (timestamped)
- Video (timestamped)
- Documents obtained
- Interview summaries
- Database search results

**5. Conclusions:**
- Assessment of findings
- Confidence levels
- Limitations of investigation
- Recommendations (if applicable)

**6. Exhibits:**
- All evidence organized
- Numbered and referenced
- Chain of custody documented

### Report Writing Standards

**Must Include:**
- Investigator identification and license
- Client authorization
- Factual, objective language
- Source attribution
- Date/time of all observations
- Photograph/video references
- Limitations and caveats

**Must Avoid:**
- Speculation
- Conclusions beyond evidence
- Legal advice
- Recommendations outside scope
- Subject's thoughts/motivations (unless quoted)
- Biased language

## Best Practices

1. **Stay Legal** - When in doubt, consult attorney
2. **Document Everything** - Assume it will be scrutinized
3. **Maintain Objectivity** - Don't develop theories too early
4. **Protect Confidentiality** - Client privilege is sacred
5. **Verify Information** - Cross-check when possible
6. **Be Discreet** - Operational security matters
7. **Continue Learning** - Laws and techniques evolve
8. **Network** - Relationships with other PIs are valuable
9. **Specialize** - Develop expertise in specific areas
10. **Carry Insurance** - E&O coverage is essential
