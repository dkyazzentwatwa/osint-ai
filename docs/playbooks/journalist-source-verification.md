# Journalist Source Verification Playbook

## Overview

This playbook provides journalists with systematic procedures for verifying sources, handling anonymous sources safely, and maintaining ethical standards while conducting investigative research.

---

## Source Verification Workflow

### Primary Source Verification (The "Three-Legged Stool")

Every source must be verified through three independent channels:

```
CHANNEL 1: Identity Verification
└── Confirm who the source claims to be

CHANNEL 2: Motivation Assessment  
└── Understand why they're providing information

CHANNEL 3: Corroboration Check
└── Verify claims through independent means
```

### Step-by-Step Verification Process

#### Phase 1: Initial Assessment (Before Meeting)

1. **Background Research**
   - Search source's name across public records
   - Check professional licensing (if applicable)
   - Review social media history
   - Search court records for credibility issues
   - Verify employment claims

2. **Digital Footprint Analysis**
   - Review online presence consistency
   - Check for conflicting information
   - Identify potential red flags
   - Assess technical sophistication

3. **Preliminary Risk Assessment**
   - Is the source who they claim to be?
   - Do they have access to claimed information?
   - Are there credibility concerns?
   - What is their potential bias?

#### Phase 2: Initial Contact

1. **Secure Communication Setup**
   - Use encrypted channels (Signal, ProtonMail)
   - Verify contact method authenticity
   - Establish secure meeting protocols
   - Document all interactions

2. **Identity Confirmation**
   - Request government-issued ID (if safe)
   - Ask specific questions only real person would know
   - Verify through third-party introduction if possible
   - Cross-check details from multiple sources

3. **Information Vetting**
   - Ask for documents before details
   - Request verifiable specifics (dates, names, locations)
   - Look for internal consistency
   - Identify what can be independently confirmed

#### Phase 3: Document Authentication

**Physical Documents:**
- Examine paper, fonts, formatting for authenticity
- Check for signs of alteration
- Verify letterheads and signatures
- Contact issuing organization directly

**Digital Documents:**
- Review metadata (creation date, author, software)
- Check file hash against known versions
- Analyze for signs of manipulation
- Verify email headers if applicable

**Photographs:**
- Analyze EXIF data
- Check for Photoshop/manipulation
- Verify location through landmarks
- Cross-reference with known images

**Audio/Video:**
- Check for editing/splicing
- Verify timestamps
- Analyze background sounds/visuals
- Confirm speaker identity

#### Phase 4: Corroboration

For each claim the source makes:

- [ ] Find at least one independent source
- [ ] Obtain documentary evidence
- [ ] Verify through official channels
- [ ] Cross-check with public records
- [ ] Consult subject matter experts

**Corroboration Failure Protocol:**
- Document what couldn't be verified
- Assess if claims are still publishable
- Determine appropriate caveats
- Consider legal consultation

## Anonymous Source Handling

### When to Grant Anonymity

**Valid Reasons:**
- Fear of retaliation or physical harm
- Risk of job loss or financial ruin
- Legal jeopardy (whistleblower protection)
- Privacy concerns for non-public figures
- National security considerations

**Invalid Reasons:**
- Avoiding embarrassment
- Concealing conflicts of interest
- Spreading misinformation safely
- Personal vendetta protection

### Anonymous Source Protocol

#### Contract with Source

Establish clear terms:

```
1. IDENTIFICATION: Source provides real identity to editor
2. CONDITIONS: Specific circumstances for anonymity
3. SCOPE: What information is covered
4. DURATION: How long anonymity lasts
5. QUOTES: Approval process for direct quotes
6. TERMINATION: When anonymity might be lifted
```

#### Identity Documentation

**Know Your Source Checklist:**

- [ ] Full legal name
- [ ] Date of birth
- [ ] Current address
- [ ] Government-issued ID (photographed)
- [ ] Professional credentials
- [ ] Reason for anonymity
- [ ] Potential conflicts of interest
- [ ] Motivation for coming forward

**Storage Requirements:**
- Secure, encrypted storage
- Limited access (editor + reporter)
- Separate from story files
- Regular access audits

#### Source Protection Measures

**Communication Security:**
- Use Signal or similar encrypted messaging
- No SMS or unencrypted email
- Regularly rotate contact methods
- Avoid patterns in communication timing

**Meeting Security:**
- Vary meeting locations
- Avoid source's home/work
- Check for surveillance
- Use counter-surveillance techniques
- Consider legal meeting spaces (attorney offices)

**Publication Safety:**
- Remove identifying details from quotes
- Alter minor identifying details (with source approval)
- Time publication strategically
- Warn source before publication
- Monitor for retaliation post-publication

## Document Authentication Procedures

### Document Verification Checklist

**Physical Documents:**

| Element | Check | Method |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Paper | Authentic period paper | UV light, watermark |
| Fonts | Period-appropriate | Font database comparison |
| Signatures | Genuine | Expert analysis, comparison |
| Letterhead | Authentic | Contact organization |
| Dates | Chronologically valid | Calendar verification |
| Watermarks | Present and correct | Backlight examination |
| Binding | Appropriate method | Expert consultation |

**Digital Documents:**

```
METADATA ANALYSIS:
- Creation date vs. claimed date
- Software used vs. claimed source
- Author field vs. claimed author
- Modification history
- Template detection

CONTENT ANALYSIS:
- Anachronistic language/terminology
- Format inconsistencies
- Internal contradictions
- Copy-paste artifacts
- Spelling/grammar patterns
```

### Email Verification

**Header Analysis:**
1. Trace full routing path
2. Verify originating IP
3. Check SPF/DKIM/DMARC
4. Validate timestamps
5. Flag suspicious relays
6. Compare with known patterns

**Content Verification:**
- Language matches sender
- Timing is appropriate
- Context makes sense
- Recipients are plausible
- Reply chains are coherent

### Photo/Video Authentication

**Technical Analysis:**
- EXIF data consistency
- Compression artifacts
- Shadow/lighting analysis
- Geolocation verification
- Weather/date correlation

**Source Verification:**
- Original photographer identity
- Chain of custody
- First publication location
- Consistency with other sources

## Fact-Checking Procedures

### The "Two-Source Rule"

Never publish based on a single source. Minimum requirements:

- **Breaking News:** 2 independent sources
- **Sensitive Allegations:** 3+ independent sources  
- **Anonymous Source Claims:** Must be corroborated
- **Document-Based Stories:** Source + document verification

### Fact-Check Workflow

```
STEP 1: Isolate Each Claim
└── Break story into individual factual assertions

STEP 2: Verify Each Claim
├── Check primary sources
├── Find secondary confirmation  
├── Consult subject matter experts
└── Document verification method

STEP 3: Flag Uncertainty
├── Note what can't be verified
├── Assess confidence level
└── Determine appropriate caveats

STEP 4: Legal Review
├── Identify potential defamation issues
├── Review fair use/fair dealing
└── Consult publication lawyer

STEP 5: Final Verification
├── Re-check all key facts
├── Verify quotes with sources
└── Confirm document authenticity
```

### Fact-Check Documentation

Maintain verification logs:

| Claim | Source | Method | Verified By | Date | Confidence |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------|------|------------|
| [Claim text] | [Source] | [How] | [Name] | [Date] | [High/Med/Low] |

## Legal Considerations

### Defamation Avoidance

**Before Publishing Allegations:**

1. **Truth Defense**
   - Absolute confidence in accuracy
   - Documentary evidence
   - Multiple corroborating sources
   - Expert verification

2. **Fair Report Privilege**
   - Accurate reporting of official proceedings
   - Neutral presentation of allegations
   - Balanced coverage of responses

3. **Opinion vs. Fact**
   - Clearly label opinions
   - Support factual claims
   - Avoid loaded language

### Reporter's Privilege

**Protecting Sources:**
- Shield laws vary by jurisdiction
- Know your state's protections
- Understand federal limitations
- Consult media lawyer before subpoena response

**Note-Taking Best Practices:**
- Date all notes
- Identify sources clearly
- Separate fact from inference
- Preserve all drafts
- Secure storage

### Copyright Considerations

**Fair Use Analysis:**
- Purpose (news reporting favors fair use)
- Nature of copyrighted work
- Amount used (minimal necessary)
- Market effect

**Document Republishing:**
- Original context when possible
- Attribution always
- Consider licensing for extensive use

## Source Protection Measures

### Physical Security

**For High-Risk Sources:**
- Vary meeting patterns
- Use secure locations
- Check for surveillance
- Consider counter-surveillance
- Have emergency protocols

### Digital Security

**Source Communication:**
- Encrypted messaging only
- No SMS for sensitive info
- Regular contact method rotation
- Secure drop options
- Dead drops for documents

**Reporter Protection:**
- Separate research/personal devices
- VPN for all research
- Encrypted storage for notes
- Regular security audits
- Training on surveillance detection

### Post-Publication Protection

**Monitor for Retaliation:**
- Watch for source mentions in media
- Monitor social media for threats
- Check legal filings naming source
- Maintain regular source check-ins

**Response Preparedness:**
- Legal defense fund awareness
- Support resources for sources
- Escalation procedures
- Media advocacy plans

## Ethical Guidelines

### Transparency with Audience

**When to Disclose:**
- Anonymous sources (explain why)
- Conflicts of interest
- Verification limitations
- Methods used
- Errors discovered post-publication

### Fairness to Subjects

**Right to Respond:**
- Contact subjects of allegations
- Provide reasonable response time
- Include responses fairly
- Correct errors promptly

**Minimize Harm:**
- Consider privacy of non-public figures
- Avoid gratuitous personal details
- Don't inflate allegations
- Be cautious with criminal accusations

## Emergency Protocols

### Source in Danger

1. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Cease all contact temporarily
   - Document threat indicators
   - Alert editor/security team
   - Contact source through backup method

2. **Support Measures:**
   - Legal referral
   - Security consultation
   - Relocation assistance info
   - Mental health resources

### Legal Threats

1. **Document Everything:**
   - Preserve all notes/recordings
   - Secure drafts and research
   - Document verification efforts

2. **Immediate Response:**
   - Alert editor/legal team
   - Do not destroy any materials
   - Cease further reporting temporarily
   - Prepare defense documentation

3. **Publication Decision:**
   - Legal review of specific claims
   - Risk/benefit analysis
   - Possible redactions
   - Timing considerations

---

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Verify, verify, verify** - Three-legged stool for every source
2. **Document everything** - Assume you'll need to defend it
3. **Protect sources** - Their trust is your most valuable asset
4. **Be transparent** - Tell readers what you know and don't know
5. **Minimize harm** - Consider consequences of publication
6. **Consult experts** - Don't guess on technical/legal matters
7. **Stay secure** - Protect yourself and your sources
8. **Correct errors** - Promptly and transparently
